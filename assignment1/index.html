Question 1

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

As I explored various websites, this one stood out among the few that truly caught my attention. After spending a few more minutes comparing different sites, I decided to go with this one. Why? What made it stand out? Upon opening the website, the animated loading screen immediately made a strong impression on me. When I saw the landing page with its striking colors, I knew I wanted to review this site. Question 5

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

I believe the primary goal of the website, as stated on the site itself, is to raise awareness about the importance and value of various professions. It aims to ensure that workers are not treated with prejudice but are instead appreciated for their labor. The website designer achieves this through effective storytelling, using different sections to build upon the message gradually. The site provides both verbal and visual information, explaining why we should respect and appreciate workers and highlighting their contributions to the world. At the end of the webpage, the designer includes a small interactive feature where users can click a heart button to make a cascade of hearts appear. This feature serves as an invitation to increase awareness and encourages users to show their appreciation for workers.

Question 6

How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

I feel that each section of the website builds upon the previous one, expanding on related arguments in a cohesive manner. By combining text and imagery, the author has made the website more engaging, encouraging users to thoroughly read and consider the content. The text is neither too long nor too short but is presented concisely, making it easier to digest. The use of animation and motion adds to the experience, making the content more dynamic and less likely to be ignored. Finally, the “click to send rays of kindness” button, which generates hearts when clicked at the end, invites users to implement the website's message in their daily lives.

Question 7

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

I feel that the design of this website is not intended for long-term use by most users, as it primarily serves as an informative page. Users might visit it only once, return occasionally, or share it with others to support its goal of raising awareness. The design is focused on capturing attention in the moment and hopes that users will remember the content they read. While the website designer aims for the message to have a lasting impact, the site lacks interactive features and personalization that might encourage users to return frequently. Instead, the design prioritizes making a strong initial impression and engaging users right away.

Question 8

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

In my opinion, the website lacks interactive experiences that would encourage users to return regularly. It does not include personalized elements or interactive activities. The main aspects that might draw users back are (1) the website’s appealing design and numerous animations, and (2) the engaging content. Although the website excels in storytelling and content, it could be made more memorable and interactive if the designer incorporated features like quizzes, personalized activities, newsletters, or surveys. These additions could enhance user engagement and provide ongoing reasons for users to revisit the site.

Question 9

What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

After checking out the website, it’s clear that the designer has put a lot of thought into using different media to get their message across. They’ve used photos and silhouettes of janitors, along with trash-related images like trash bins and sweeping tools. These elements really help highlight the importance of the work being showcased.

The site also features realistic elements like trees, buildings, and benches, and even includes a landmark like the Taj Mahal. This mix of real-world imagery helps emphasize the value of all kinds of work.

To keep things feeling fresh and down-to-earth, the designer has added some cool styling touches. Scribbles, paper textures, and ripped paper effects give it a less corporate vibe. Plus, the use of photographs and painting frames adds a personal touch. All these elements work together to make the website engaging and memorable.

Question 10

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

The references on the website give us a clear idea of how to engage with the content. For example:

Texture and Style The use of paper textures and scribbles makes the site feel more personal and laid-back. This helps users connect with the content in a less formal way.

Real-Life Imagery With photos and objects like trash bins and cleaning tools, the designer makes it clear that the issues discussed are real and relevant. This encourages us to take the message seriously and think about it in our everyday lives.

Attention to Detail The focus on details helps us recognize and appreciate the hard work and skills of people in different jobs. It shows that every profession matters and deserves respect.

All these elements work together to make the website engaging and thought-provoking, prompting us to value the contributions of workers everywhere.

Question 11

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

Christina’s website guides us on how to feel through its design and references. By using real-life structures like buildings, trees, and even the Taj Mahal, she shows that the issue is something big and important, not just a small, isolated problem.
